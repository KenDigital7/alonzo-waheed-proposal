# Living Life Balance — Proposal Brief

## Client Overview

**Client:** Alonzo Waheed  
**Brand:** Living Life Balance  
**Business Type:** Personal brand, wellness coaching, speaking, and digital education  
**Current Digital Presence:** Static website (no booking, no monetization); social handles exist but are unoptimized and unsynchronized  
**Target Launch:** End of March

---

## The Core Problem

Alonzo has spent years building credibility doing the work — CBT-certified resilience curricula delivered across 30+ Chicago Public Schools, executive orders, keynotes at major churches and community organizations, Martin Luther King Day addresses, and sustained advocacy at the intersection of mental health, spiritual wellness, and civic engagement. Nearly all of it was done under or alongside his current organization, EAT. The result: he walks into rooms where people say "that's EAT" — not "that's Alonzo Waheed."

The assets exist. The online courses are ready. The book is written. The workshop curricula are proven. Audiences are already asking where they can book him. The problem is the infrastructure to make all of it findable, schedulable, and paid — and a brand identity that makes clear the practitioner is Alonzo, not the organization.

---

## Brand Positioning

Living Life Balance is not separate from Alonzo — it is the expression of how he lives the philosophy he teaches. The brand's core claim is practitioner authenticity: Alonzo documents his own balance publicly, showing the therapy, the physical training, the Black-owned spaces he frequents, the stages he speaks on, and the community work he does. The brand says: *this is what it looks like when a Black man actually lives what he teaches.*

The separation from EAT happens not by distancing from that credibility — that credibility is Alonzo's. The move is to show the world that the work exists because of who Alonzo is, not where he works.

> Core positioning line: "Alonzo Waheed is Living Life Balance — a practitioner who lives publicly what he teaches professionally."

---

## Narrative Hook

The story starts at the return from Africa and the Restore Fellowship experience. This is the natural bridge. From restored — to balanced — to building the brand that reflects the work. Alonzo lost his camera in Africa; he's starting fresh, documenting it now, and that human detail makes the launch feel earned rather than manufactured.

The campaign and content arc for launch: **"From Restored to Balanced."**

This narrative works across all platforms:
- LinkedIn: Expert reflection on what restoration means for Black professional men and community leaders
- Instagram: Organic daily documentation of what living balanced actually looks like
- YouTube: The journey as a long-form record — the speaking, the training, the healing

---

## Service Pillars

**1. Speaking Engagements & Keynotes**  
Target audiences: schools, churches, underserved community organizations, educational boards, and civic/legislative spaces. Longer horizon: corporations addressing burnout and resilience. This is Alonzo's highest-revenue-potential service at launch. Credentials: Martin Luther King Day keynotes, city-wide school programs, Community Renewal Society work.

**2. Workshops & Training**  
Specialized curriculum built around resilience, burnout prevention, and mental/spiritual health. Dedicated programming for Black men — addressing depression, direction, and standard-setting. CBT-certified. Proven at scale across CPS turnaround schools.

**3. Digital Educational Products**  
Online courses and e-books that are ready to sell right now and have no buying pathway. Monetized through the website (Shopify or equivalent product integration). This becomes a passive revenue layer alongside active speaking income.

**4. Direct Coaching**  
One-on-one and group coaching engagements. Premium access to Alonzo as a practitioner. Positioned as the high-touch option for those who want ongoing personal guidance.

**5. Media & Literature**  
Book distribution and long-form written content now. Short film development later — once the brand has produced revenue and the documented story provides the source material for production. The film is the full expression of the arc that begins at launch.

---

## Archival Authority Strategy

Alonzo's credibility predates this launch by years. The work happened — executive orders, school attendance improvements, legislative presentations, media appearances. The fact that it wasn't documented in real-time doesn't mean it can't be established now. The launch is an opportunity to retroactively chronicle this history: introduce who Alonzo is, what he's built, and then position everything going forward as the next chapter.

This archival authority becomes the platform foundation — proof that he isn't starting from zero, he's just now building the stage the work deserves.

---

## Digital Infrastructure Needs

**Website Rebuild**  
Modernize the Living Life Balance site from a static page into a working business tool. Must include:
- Backend scheduling/booking system for speaking engagements
- Shopify or integrated product store for courses and books
- Consistent visual brand across all pages

**Social Media Ecosystem**  
Synchronized presence across LinkedIn, Instagram, YouTube, and Facebook — same brand aesthetic, complementary strategies per platform:

- **LinkedIn:** Expert positioning through articles and op-eds; audiences are lawmakers, school boards, educators, corporate decision-makers
- **Instagram:** Organic, daily-life storytelling; therapy visits, training, Black-owned businesses, community events — the "how" behind living balanced
- **YouTube:** Long-form content repository for speaking engagements and coursework; used as pitch material when booking speaking gigs

**Content Backbone**  
Weekly recap posts (Friday or Saturday): consistent cadence regardless of what else is published that week. This is the minimum viable content commitment to keep the brand active and credible without requiring daily production.

## Tiered Launch Roadmap

**Tier 1 — Foundation (Launch Phase)**  
_Target: End of March_  
- Website booking/scheduling system live — speaking revenue can begin immediately  
- Social media handles synced; consistent brand visual across all platforms  
- "From Restored to Balanced" storytelling campaign launches  
- Existing online courses connected to the website and monetized  

**Tier 2 — Growth & Maintenance**  
- Weekly recap content strategy in place (Friday/Saturday posts)  
- Substack launched for long-form written thought leadership (diversified platform; not reliant on Instagram alone)  
- Archival authority content published — past wins documented and attributed to Alonzo  

**Tier 3 — Full Production**  
_Contingent on revenue stability_  
- Professional media coverage for major speaking events  
- Short film development begins as brand revenue provides production budget

## Investment Framing

This engagement is structured as a **project-based launch agreement** — not a long-term retainer. The explicit goal is to get Alonzo's core revenue-generating assets live quickly so the business begins to sustain itself. No one is locking in to a recurring commitment before either side can see how the business performs.

The first phase is intentionally narrow: booking infrastructure, monetized courses, and a synchronized social presence that establishes brand authority. Once that's live and revenue is moving, both parties evaluate together. If the business is growing, the conversation about ongoing support happens from a position of demonstrated results — not hope and obligation.

The framing in the proposal should reflect this directly, because Alonzo was transparent about it: he wants to make sure he can pay for the work with the revenue the work generates. That transparency deserves to be met with a matching structure.
